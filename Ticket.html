Ticket / IC Card

Kyoto, known for its rich history and cultural heritage, offers an efficient and convenient transportation system for both locals and tourists alike. When exploring this enchanting city, visitors can choose between using tickets or IC cards for fare payment. The ticket system allows for single-use fares, making it suitable for those on short stays or infrequent travelers. On the other hand, the IC card, such as the widely-used ICOCA card, offers a reloadable option, providing a seamless experience for frequent riders. With various transport options, including buses and trains, navigating Kyoto becomes a breeze, allowing visitors to immerse themselves fully in the city's awe-inspiring temples, gardens, and traditional streets.

How to Buy a Ticket

Step 1: To purchase a ticket, refer to the route map displayed above the ticket machine to find the current fare from your current station to your final destination.

Step 2: Look for a "Multifunctional Ticket Vending Machine".

Step 3: Select the English language or any other language you prefer if available.

Step 4: Select the Ticket to buy the ticket.

Step 5: Choose that amount from the displayed amounts on the machine.

Step 6: Next display will be like this which ask you to make the payment.

Step 7: insert the appropriate amount (using bills and/or coins) for the displayed fare to purchase a ticket.

Step 8: Take the ticket and any change that is returned by the machine. Tickets can be purchased for any station displayed in the fare table on the ticket vending machine.

Step 5: Head for the ticket gate with your ticket in hand and insert it into the slot in the gate.
* Please note that some ticket gates are only for IC cards.

How to Buy a IC Card

Step 1: Look for a "Multifunctional Ticket Vending Machine".

Step 3: Select the English language or any other language you prefer if available.

Step 4: Select Purchase New Suica (e.g for the IC Card).

Step 5: Choose any amount from the displayed amounts on the machine.
* Please note 500 yen will be carged inorder to by the IC Card.
For example, inserting 2,000 yen into the ticket vending machine when purchasing a card will charge the card with 1,500 yen to be used for train fare or shopping.

Step 7: Insert the appropriate amount (using bills and/or coins) for the displayed fare to purchase a card.

Step 8: Take the IC Card and any change that is returned by the machine. IC Card can be purchased for any station displayed in the fare table on the ticket vending machine.

Step 5: To use the card for riding the train or bus, simply touch the card to the card-recognition area on the automated ticket gate.

How to Charge a IC Card

Step 1: Look for a "Multifunctional Ticket Vending Machine".

Step 3: Select the English language or any other language you prefer if available.

Step 4: Select Charge.

Step 5: Select the amount to charge.
*Up to 20,000 yen can be charged to a card.
*Screens show charging with an automated ticket machine.

Step 7: Insert the appropriate amount (using bills and/or coins) for the displayed fare to charge the card.

Step 8: Take the IC Card and any change that is returned by the machine. IC Card can be charged for any station displayed in the fare table on the ticket vending machine.

Step 5: To use the card for riding the train or bus, simply touch the card to the card-recognition area on the automated ticket gate.
